# Obsidian (software)

**Obsidian** is a [personal knowledge management](https://example.com/wiki/Personal_knowledge_management "Personal knowledge management") and [note-taking](https://example.com/wiki/Note-taking "Note-taking") [software application](https://example.com/wiki/Application_software "Application software") that operates on [Markdown](https://example.com/wiki/Markdown "Markdown") files.[^2] It allows users to make internal links for notes and then to visualize the connections as a graph. It is designed to help users organize and structure their thoughts and knowledge in a flexible, non-linear way.[^3] The software is free for personal use, with commercial licenses and hosted services such as [Obsidian Publish](https://example.com/wiki/Obsidian_Publish "Obsidian Publish") and [Obsidian Sync](https://example.com/wiki/Obsidian_Sync "Obsidian Sync") available as paid add-ons.[^4]

Obsidian is developed by Dynalist Inc., a company founded by [Shida Li](https://example.com/wiki/Shida_Li "Shida Li") and Erica Xu. The application stores notes as plain-text Markdown files in a local folder called a "vault", allowing users full control over their data.[^5] It supports a wide range of [plugins](https://example.com/wiki/Plug-in_\(computing\) "Plug-in (computing)") and themes created by its [open-source](https://example.com/wiki/Open-source_software "Open-source software") community.[^6]

## History

Obsidian was created by Shida Li and Erica Xu, who previously developed the outliner application [Dynalist](https://example.com/wiki/Dynalist "Dynalist").[^7] The first public version was released in March 2020 as a desktop application for [Windows](https://example.com/wiki/Windows "Windows"), [macOS](https://example.com/wiki/MacOS "MacOS"), and [Linux](https://example.com/wiki/Linux "Linux").[^8] The application quickly gained a following among users interested in the [Zettelkasten](https://example.com/wiki/Zettelkasten "Zettelkasten") method and [personal knowledge management](https://example.com/wiki/Personal_knowledge_management "Personal knowledge management") workflows.[^9]

Mobile versions for [iOS](https://example.com/wiki/IOS "IOS") and [Android](https://example.com/wiki/Android_\(operating_system\) "Android (operating system)") were released in 2021.[^10] In October 2022, the application reached version 1.0, which included a new default theme, improved tab support, and a redesigned settings interface.[^11]

Obsidian introduced [Obsidian Publish](https://example.com/wiki/Obsidian_Publish "Obsidian Publish"), a paid service for publishing notes as a website, and [Obsidian Sync](https://example.com/wiki/Obsidian_Sync "Obsidian Sync"), an [end-to-end encrypted](https://example.com/wiki/End-to-end_encryption "End-to-end encryption") synchronization service.[^12]

## Features

Obsidian stores all notes as plain-text [Markdown](https://example.com/wiki/Markdown "Markdown") files in a local folder on the user's device, referred to as a "vault".[^13] This approach gives users complete ownership of their data and allows notes to be opened with any text editor.

### Linking and graph view

A core feature of Obsidian is its support for [internal links](https://example.com/wiki/Internal_link "Internal link") between notes using a `[[wikilink]]` syntax.[^14] These links create a network of connected notes that can be visualized through a built-in graph view.[^15]

### Plugins

Obsidian supports an extensive [plugin](https://example.com/wiki/Plug-in_\(computing\) "Plug-in (computing)") system that allows third-party developers to extend the application's functionality.[^16] As of 2024, the community plugin repository contains over 1,500 plugins.[^17]

## Reception

Obsidian has received generally favorable reviews from technology publications. *[The Verge](https://example.com/wiki/The_Verge "The Verge")* praised the application for its flexibility and local-first approach, noting that "Obsidian is a powerful note-taking app that gives you full control of your data."[^2] *[PC Magazine](https://example.com/wiki/PC_Magazine "PC Magazine")* awarded it an Editors' Choice designation, highlighting its linking capabilities and customizability, though noting a steep learning curve for new users.[^3]

The application has been compared to other note-taking tools such as [Notion](https://example.com/wiki/Notion_\(productivity_software\) "Notion (productivity software)"), [Roam Research](https://example.com/wiki/Roam_Research "Roam Research"), and [Logseq](https://example.com/wiki/Logseq "Logseq"). Commentators have noted that Obsidian differentiates itself through its local-first storage model and emphasis on user data ownership.[^18] However, critics have pointed out that the [proprietary](https://example.com/wiki/Proprietary_software "Proprietary software") nature of the application itself limits user freedom compared to fully [open-source](https://example.com/wiki/Open-source_software "Open-source software") alternatives.[^19]
