# Quadratic Formula

In algebra, the **quadratic formula** gives the solutions of the general quadratic equation. Given a second-degree polynomial of the form ![ax^{2}+bx+c=0](https://example.com/math/quad.svg), the values of *x* satisfying this equation are given by the quadratic formula.

The formula is expressed as:

![x={\frac {-b\pm {\sqrt {b^{2}-4ac}}}{2a}}](https://example.com/math/quadformula.svg)

The quantity under the square root, ![b^{2}-4ac](https://example.com/math/disc.svg), is known as the **discriminant**. Its value determines the nature of the solutions.
