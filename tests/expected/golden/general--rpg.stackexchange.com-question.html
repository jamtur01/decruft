I can only speak to editions 4 onward...

4th Ed:

  • attributes on 3d6
  • Attributes ST DX CN IQ LK CH
  • Types: Warrior, Wizard, Rogue
  • Weapon damages presume 1d for daggers, 0D unarmed
  • Combat Adds on St, Dex, Lk
  • Rogues have a level limit; at cap, change class to warrior or wizard
  • No spell schools
  • Level by total EP
  • Saving rolls presumed to be LK; other stats optional
  • Saving rolls fail on natural rolls of 3 (⚀⚁) and 4 (⚀⚂) that aren't doubles

5th Edition:

  • attributes on 3d6
  • Attributes ST DX CN IQ LK CH and optionally SP
  • Types: Warrior, Wizard, Rogue, Warrior-Wizard
  • Weapon damages presume 1d for daggers, 0D unarmed
  • Combat Adds on St, Dex, Lk
  • No spell schools
  • Weapon damages hit "modern" levels; 1d unarmed, 2d daggers.
  • Level by total EP
  • Saving rolls presumed to be LK; other stats optional but encouraged
  • Saving rolls fail on natural rolls of 3 (⚀⚁) and 4 (⚀⚂) that aren't doubles

5.5 Edition

  • attributes on 3d6 TARO
  • Attributes ST DX CN IQ LK CH and optionally SP
  • Types: Warrior, Wizard, Rogue, Warrior-Wizard
  • Weapon damages presume 1d for daggers, 0D unarmed
  • Combat Adds on St, Dex, Lk
  • No spell schools
  • Talents optional
  • Level by total EP
  • Saving rolls presumed to be LK; other stats optional but encouraged
  • Saving rolls fail on natural rolls of 3 (⚀⚁) and 4 (⚀⚂) that aren't doubles

7.0 Main book

  • attributes on 3d6 TARO
  • Attributes ST DX CN IQ LK CH WZ SP
  • Types: Warrior, Wizard, Rogue, Paragon, Citizen, Specialist (Specialist Mage, Leader, Ranger)
  • Weapon damages presume 2d for daggers, 1D unarmed
  • Combat Adds on St, Dex, Lk
  • spell schools listed
  • Talents optional, different from 5.5
  • Level by highest of 4 attributes by type; EP raise attributes
  • Saving rolls now on any attribute.
  • Saving rolls fail on natural rolls of 3 (⚀⚁)

7.5 Main book

  • attributes on 3d6 TARO
  • Attributes ST DX CN IQ LK CH WZ SP
  • Types: Warrior, Wizard, Rogue, Paragon, Citizen, Specialist (Specialist Mage, Leader, Ranger)
  • Weapon damages presume 1d for daggers, 0D unarmed
  • Combat Adds on St, Dex, Lk
  • spell schools listed
  • Talents optional, different from 5.5 and 7.0 (but close to 7.0)
  • Level by highest of 4 attributes by type; EP raise attributes
  • Saving rolls fail on natural rolls of 3 (⚀⚁)

Deluxe

  • attributes on 3d6 TARO
  • Attributes ST DX CN IQ LK CH WZ SP
  • Types: Warrior, Wizard, Rogue, Paragon, Citizen,
  • Weapon damages presume 1d for daggers, 0D unarmed
  • Combat Adds on St, Dex, Lk
  • spell schools listed
  • Talents optional, different from 5.5 and 7.0 (but close to 7.0), and can be (with very narrow limits) raised directly with EP.
  • Level by highest of 4 attributes by type; EP raise attributes and talent bonuses.
  • Extensive world background
  • Saving rolls fail on natural rolls of 3 (⚀⚁)

A key difference is that in versions 7 and above, monsters don’t lose attack dice as their MR is reduced — just the adds, which as usual are 1/2 of the current monster rating (MR).

In 5E, a monster with an MR of 100 that takes 90 damage (down to 10 MR) will drop from 11d6 plus 50 adds, to 2D6 plus 5 adds. But in 7E and above, it will get its original 11D6, plus 5 adds.

This makes monsters somewhat more difficult to finish off, since they don’t lose attack dice as they take damage.

There are a few errors in the Deluxe section in the answer from aramis, but I can't reply to it directly because I'm new here.

  • Daggers are not 1D - the weakest dagger listed in the Daggers section is 2D, while the strongest is 2D+5.
  • Unarmed combat is not 0D. It is 1D * the kindred's STR multiplier. So a Midgardian Dwarf with a *2 STR multiplier, gets 2D in unarmed. (For warriors, add their warrior bonus of 1D per level as well.)
  • Combat adds are on STR, DEX, LK, and SPD as well. (SPD was left out above.)
  • Level is not determined by highest of 4 attributes. It is determined by highest of all 8 attributes. If your highest attribute is 20, you're level 2. If your highest is 36, you're level 3, and so on. Doesn't matter which attribute.

I, unfortunately, have not played T&T and am unable to provide a full documentation of the patch notes. However, here are some of the main differences between 5 and 7.5 based on information I can gather, and a quick read through the rule books for both.

System differences

  • 7.5 contains separate magic casting and magic resistance stats, 5 uses Strength as the magical casting stat
  • 7.5 contains more classes
  • 7.5 uses a modified levelling system
  • 7.5 introduces a "talent" system, as well as a more solid grasp of the "Stunts" system
  • 7.5 is designed in general to be more structured
  • Saving rolls are calculated differently between the two editions, but the final result is the same
  • 7.5 has more lenient systems for encumbrance, wielding weapons that are over weight and movement calculations
  • 5.5 has more dynamic monster behaviour laid out in the rulebook, including reactions to players beyond attack till death

Non system based differences

  • 7.5 seems to assume a high degree of knowledge in it's rulebook of previous editions of the game
  • From what I can gather the community seems to deem 5.5 as the superior version

Some more information is available here, Tunnels & Trolls 5.5 vs 7.5, as this is one of the best articles on the subject I found.

Spite damage

None of the other answers have referred to spite damage.

The fourth and fifth editions contain no mention of spite damage.

The seventh edition, on page 69, says that spite damage is a 1 point damage inflicted by either, or both, side(s), for each and every 6 rolled, during a combat round/turn.

However, while the fifth edition makes no mention of spite damage, I definitely remember using the spite damage rule circa 1981/1982, whilst using a battered fifth edition.

The concept of spite damage originated from an article, by Roy Cram, in issue 13 (late 1981) of Sorcerer's Apprentice, on page 6. So, edition 5.5 probably also contained this rule. However, I am unable to verify that it is actually present in 5.5, as I do not own a copy of that particular edition.

Nevertheless, it would seem that my assumption is correct after having found the following quote. It is worth noting, that in 5.5 the spite damage is handled differently, with respect to edition 7, if this article, Tunnels and Trolls... 5.5 vs. 7.0, is to be believed:

in 5.5, the loser alone does spite

FWIW, this article, Tunnels & Trolls 5.5 vs 7.5, appears to not acknowledge that spite damage was actually present in 5.5. Possibly, they got the rules editions confused with edition 5 (which is easily done as they are almost identical)?

In both the Mythical 6th edition, on page 30, and the earlier 2001 version of the 6th edition, on page 26, spite damage is mentioned, but it is unclear if it is just the loser who gets to use it, or both parties.

Battle

To expand upon Jeff Thompson's answer, it should be noted that prior to the fifth edition, monster adds were 50% of MR in the first round, but subsequent rounds (without resting or taking a breather), the monster adds were just 25% of MR.

If a rest occurred mid-battle (for whatever reason), then the next round thereafter, the monster's adds would go back up to MR/2, for one round, and, again, on subsequent rounds the monster's adds would go back down to MR/4, and so on... until the end of the battle.

From section 1.6 Creating Monsters of the fifth edition of the rules:

(The original method was to give the monster half its MR in adds for only the first round of fighting, and ¼ thereafter. Use either method, which suits you, although the new method is preferred.)

...

By the original method, on the next immediate fight he gets only 88 adds (I round up for almost everything). If he has time to rest between fights, he'll start out with 175 adds again.

It should be noted that there is an apparent typo on that same page, as the balrog is initially stated to have an MR of 250, resulting in adds of 125:

For example: Balrog Maximus Meany has a MR of 250. He gets 26 dice and 125 adds when he fights.

However, in the section quoted above, it is as if the balrog should have an MR of 350, which gives the quoted adds of 175 (and the subsequent adds of 88).

That means that the differences in battles, throughout the editions, could be summed up thusly:

Pre-5th editions

For each round calculate:

  • Dice=INT(MR/10) +1
  • HP=MR

First round only:

  • Adds=INT(MR/2)

Subsequent rounds:

  • Adds=INT(MR/4)

Fifth edition

For each round calculate:

  • Dice=INT(MR/10) +1
  • Adds=INT(MR/2)
  • HP=MR

Seventh edition

At beginning of battle:

  • Dice=INT(MR/10) +1

For each round calculate:

  • Adds=INT(MR/2)
  • HP=MR
